**Overview:**
This analysis examines sales performance across the United States for a retail superstore operating in 49 states.
The report explores key trends related to sales distribution, profit contribution, customer segments, shipping modes, product categories, and regional performance during the period 2014–2017.

**Customer Segments:**

The company serves three main customer segments:

Consumer – the largest contributor to total sales

Corporate – second in sales volume

Home Office – third in sales

This ranking indicates that the Consumer segment represents the primary target audience and drives most of the company’s revenue.

**Shipping Modes:**

The business offers four shipping modes:

Standard Class – most frequently used and generates the highest sales

Second Class

First Class

Same Day

The dominance of the Standard Class suggests that customers prioritize cost efficiency over delivery speed.

**Regional Performance:**

The United States market is divided into four main regions:

Region	Order Share
West	32%
East	28%
Central	23%
South	16%

The Western region leads with the highest order volume, followed by the East.
Top performing states include California, New York, Texas, Washington, and Pennsylvania, collectively contributing the majority of total sales.

**Product Categories & Profitability:**

Orders are primarily classified into three categories:

Category	    Order Share	    Sales Share  	Profit Share
Office Supplies 	60%	           31%	           42.8%
Furniture	        21%	           32%	            6%
Technology	        18%	           36%	           50.8%

Despite Office Supplies being the most frequently ordered category, Technology products generate the highest sales and profit.
**Insight: The Technology category has fewer orders but the highest revenue and profitability, making it the store’s most valuable segment.**

**Time-Based Trends**

Between 2014 and early 2015, sales of Office Supplies and Technology decreased slightly, while Furniture sales increased.

From mid-2015 onward, all categories showed significant growth, particularly Technology and Office Supplies.

Overall sales and profit trends (2014–2017) show a clear upward trajectory — indicating a promising business performance.

Seasonality: The second half of each year consistently records higher sales, especially during holiday seasons.

**Profitability Insights**

80% of all orders result in a profit.

18% of orders generate losses.

0.65% are neutral (no profit or loss).
This reflects a healthy overall profit margin and an efficient pricing strategy.

**Shipping Duration**

Most orders are delivered within 2 to 4 days, which aligns with customer expectations and supports repeat purchases.

**Top Performers**

Top 10 Products – ranked by total sales and visualized in the report.

Top 10 Sub-Categories – presented by both order volume and profitability.

Top 10 Customers by Order Count – candidates for loyalty rewards or discounts.

Top 10 Customers by Total Sales Value – potential for premium customer relationship programs.

**Key Insights Summary**

The Technology category drives the highest profitability despite lower order frequency.

The Western region is the leading market in both order count and total sales.

Standard Class shipping dominates, suggesting cost sensitivity among customers.

Year-end seasons (Q4) consistently record the highest sales.

Most customers are consumers, making them the primary focus for future marketing campaigns.

**Recommendations**

Increase focus on Technology products through marketing and stock prioritization.

Promote high-value customers with loyalty or discount programs.

Investigate loss-making orders (18%) to optimize pricing or reduce shipping costs.

Leverage seasonal patterns to plan targeted promotions in the second half of each year.

Expand operations in high-performing Western states to strengthen sales leadership.
